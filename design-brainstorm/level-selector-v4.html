◆ First Year
First Year
Experience: Completed beginner course or equivalent — approx. 0–1 year

You have taken your first steps in tango and understand the embrace. This course builds your foundation with the cross-body system, clear walking technique, and the essential building blocks that everything else in tango is built on.

What you will work on
  • ✓Cross-system walking & weight transfers
  • ✓Forward and back Ochos
  • ✓The cross (La Cruzada)
  • ✓Molinete / Giro (basic turn)
  • ✓Connection and embrace quality
  • ✓Navigating the line of dance

◆ Second Year
Second Year
Experience: First Year completed — approx. 1–2 years

Your basics are solid and you are ready to expand your vocabulary. This level introduces pattern combinations, refined connection, and the beginnings of improvisation within the milonga setting.

What you will work on
  • ✓Extended Ocho patterns & timing
  • ✓Pauses, rebounds, and syncopations
  • ✓Sacadas (leader & follower)
  • ✓Calesita and circular movements
  • ✓Musicality: phrase & rhythm awareness
  • ✓Floorcraft in a milonga

◆ Second Year PLUS
Second Year PLUS
Experience: Second Year completed — approx. 2–3 years

You are comfortable at milongas and want to deepen expression and complexity. This level explores intricate sequences, extended musicality, and the refinement that separates competent dancers from truly musical ones.

What you will work on
  • ✓Complex multi-step sequences
  • ✓Enrosque & Amague
  • ✓Musicality: interpretation & phrasing
  • ✓Turns with embellishments
  • ✓Refined leading & following sensitivity
  • ✓Stylistic exploration (Salon / Nuevo)

◆ Intermediate
Intermediate / Advanced
Experience: 4+ years of regular tango practice

An experienced dancer looking to work at the highest level of technique and artistry. This course covers the full vocabulary of Argentine tango including high-impact embellishments, alternative rhythms, and deep partner communication.

What you will work on
  • ✓Boleos (linear & circular)
  • ✓Ganchos and leg wraps
  • ✓Vals timing & style
  • ✓Milonga rhythm & traspie
  • ✓High-level improvisation
  • ✓Stage elements & performance quality
