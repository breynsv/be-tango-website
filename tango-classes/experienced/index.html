Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know which level is right for me?

+

First Year: You've completed a beginner course and are comfortable with basic steps and embrace.

Second Year: You've been dancing for 1-2 years and can navigate the dance floor with basic patterns.

Second Year PLUS: You have 2-3 years of experience and want to refine your technique and musicality.

Intermediate/Advanced: You have 4+ years of experience and are ready for advanced techniques and deep musical work.

Still not sure? Contact us or book a free trial class - we'll help you find the right level!

Do I need a dance partner to join?

+

No dance partner is required! We rotate partners during class so everyone gets to practice both roles and dance with different partners. This is one of the best ways to improve your skills.

If you do have a partner, you're welcome to attend together, but we still encourage rotation for optimal learning.

Can I try a class before committing to the full package?

+

Absolutely! We offer free trial classes for all levels. This gives you a chance to experience our teaching style, meet the instructors, and see if the level is right for you.

Book your free trial class here.

What if I miss a class?

+

Life happens! If you miss a class, you can make it up by attending another class at your level during the same trimester, subject to space availability.

We also provide class notes and practice recommendations so you can catch up on what you missed.

Can I attend multiple levels?

+

Yes! Many of our experienced dancers attend multiple levels to get more practice time and exposure to different concepts. We offer discounted rates for attending multiple classes per week.

Contact us to discuss a custom package that fits your goals.

What should I wear to class?

+

Wear comfortable clothing that allows you to move freely. For footwear, we recommend:

  • Tango shoes (if you have them)
  • Leather-soled dance shoes
  • Clean indoor shoes with smooth soles

Avoid sneakers or rubber-soled shoes as they can stick to the floor and limit your movement.
