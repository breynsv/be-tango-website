About Our Woluwe Location

Dance in the heart of Woluwe-Saint-Pierre at GC Kontakt, a beautiful community center housed in a historical carriage building.

Our cosy dance studio offers the perfect atmosphere for learning Argentine tango. Whether you're taking your first steps or refining advanced technique, you'll feel right at home in this welcoming space.

Getting There

  • By Metro: Take line 1 to Stockel station, then a short walk
  • By Tram: Line 39 to Ban-Eik stop
  • By Car: Free parking available behind the main building and in surrounding streets

Facility Highlights

Frequently Asked Questions

What's special about the Woluwe location?

+

Our Woluwe location is housed in GC Kontakt, a beautiful community center in a historical carriage building. The cosy dance studio provides an intimate and welcoming atmosphere perfect for learning tango. Plus, with excellent public transport links and free parking, it's incredibly convenient to reach.

Is parking available at GC Kontakt?

+

Yes, absolutely! There's free parking available behind the main building and plenty of street parking in the surrounding area. You won't have any trouble finding a spot when you come to class.

How do I get there by public transport?

+

The location is very accessible by public transport. Take Metro line 1 to Stockel station, or Tram 39 to the Ban-Eik stop. Both are a short walk from GC Kontakt at Avenue Orban 54.

Which level should I choose?

+

If you're brand new to tango, start with our Beginners class on Thursday at 19:30. If you have some experience, we offer First Years (Level 1+), Second Years (Level 2), Second Years PLUS (Level 2+), and Intermediate/Advanced (Level 3+). Contact us if you're unsure which level is right for you - we're happy to advise!

Can I try a class before committing?

+

Absolutely! Your first lesson is completely free with no commitment required. This gives you a chance to experience our teaching style, meet the community, and see if tango is for you. Book your free trial today!

Do I need to bring a dance partner?

+

No, you don't need to bring a partner. We rotate partners during class so everyone gets to dance with different people. This is actually beneficial as it helps you adapt to different dance styles and improves your lead/follow skills.

What's the difference between Woluwe and Brussels locations?

+

Both locations offer high-quality instruction from the same experienced teachers. The main differences are the class schedules and venue atmosphere. Woluwe (GC Kontakt) offers a cosy, community-center feel with Tuesday and Thursday classes, while Brussels (Rue du Marais) has Monday and Wednesday classes. Choose based on what's most convenient for your schedule and location.
