Argentine tango classes
for beginners in Brussels

Thinking about starting Argentine tango in Brussels but not sure what to expect? No partner, no experience, no idea what to wear — that's exactly where most of our students start. Here's everything you actually need to know before your first class.

What to expect at your first tango class

Your first Argentine tango class at BE-TANGO is not a performance. Nobody expects you to arrive knowing anything. The room is warm, the music is on, and the only thing you need to do is show up.

In a typical beginners class, we start from the very beginning: how to stand, how to walk with intention, how to hold your partner in the abrazo (the tango embrace). We don't rush into steps or figures. The first session is about your posture, your balance, and learning to move with someone else — that's already the heart of tango.

What you'll notice almost immediately is the atmosphere. Our beginners at BE-TANGO come from all walks of life — office workers, retirees, students, people who've never danced a day in their life. The mood is relaxed and encouraging. Sonja and Sven have been teaching tango in Brussels since 2007, and that experience shows in how they structure a class: patient, precise, and always a bit of fun thrown in.

If you can walk, you can learn Argentine tango.

By the end of your first class, you'll have a real sense of the connection that makes tango so addictive. It's not about the steps. It's about what happens between two people when the music starts.

You don't need a partner — here's why

This is by far the most common question we get: "Do I need to bring someone?" The answer is a clear no. The vast majority of students who walk through our door in Brussels arrive on their own, and that's genuinely fine.

In our group classes, we rotate partners throughout the session. This is not just a logistical convenience — it's actually one of the most effective ways to learn tango. When you dance with different partners, you develop real adaptability. You can't rely on knowing your partner's habits. You have to listen, communicate through the embrace, and respond in the moment. That's what turns a beginner into a social dancer.

The social aspect is another reason to come alone. Classes at BE-TANGO are genuinely convivial. You meet people, you laugh, you make connections. Many students who arrived solo have found regular dance partners through the group — some have gone on to dance together at milongas for years.

  • No partner? No problem. Partner rotation is built into every class.
  • Coming as a couple? You're welcome, but we'll still ask you to rotate — it makes both of you better dancers.
  • Social anxiety? Completely normal. Everyone's in the same boat at the start.

Tango is one of the few social activities in Brussels where showing up alone is actually the norm, not the exception. You won't feel out of place.

What to wear and where to go

For your first class, don't overthink clothing. Comfortable clothes that let you move freely — trousers, leggings, a relaxed top. You don't need a special outfit. What matters is that you're not stiff or self-conscious about what you're wearing.

Shoes are the one thing worth paying attention to. Avoid rubber soles — they grip the floor and put strain on your knees. What you want is a smooth leather sole, or a flat shoe that lets your foot slide slightly. City shoes, leather-soled flats, or dance shoes all work well. If you genuinely have nothing suitable, thick socks on a wooden floor work for a trial class.

As for location, BE-TANGO teaches at two Brussels locations:

  • Brussels Centre: Rue du Marais 68, 1000 Brussels — five minutes from Rogier and Botanique metro stations (lines 2 & 6). Free parking available nearby at Rue aux Choux 64.
  • Woluwe-Saint-Lambert: Avenue Orban 54, 1150 Woluwe-Saint-Pierre — free parking directly in front. Ideal if you're coming from the east of Brussels or the surrounding area.

How long until you can dance socially?

The honest answer: most beginners are ready for their first milonga after around 3 to 6 months of regular classes — typically once or twice a week. That's not a guarantee, but it's a realistic benchmark based on what we see at BE-TANGO every year.

The journey usually goes like this: you start with group classes, where you build your foundational vocabulary — the walk, the embrace, basic figures, musicality. After a few months, you start attending our practica evenings — relaxed practice sessions where you can repeat what you've learned without the pressure of a structured class. The practica is the bridge between class and the milonga.

The milonga itself — a social tango dance event — is where tango comes fully alive. You ask someone to dance with a glance across the room (the cabeceo), you share a tanda (a set of 3–4 songs), and the whole conversation happens without a single word. It's a unique experience, and getting there is absolutely within reach for anyone who starts today.

At BE-TANGO, the path is clear: beginners group classes, practica sessions, optional private lessons to accelerate your progress, and specialist workshops throughout the year. You don't need to figure it out alone.

Start with a free trial class

The best way to know if tango is for you is to try it — which is exactly why BE-TANGO offers a free trial class with no strings attached. No registration fee, no commitment, no awkwardness at the end if it's not your thing.

The trial class is a full, real beginner session. You'll learn alongside other newcomers, get a feel for the teaching style, see the studio space, and experience the music. It's the complete picture — not a watered-down taster.

If you love it (and most people do), you can join the Start-to-Tango cycle, our structured 14-class beginners programme. New cycles start in September and February. Places fill up, so if you're interested, booking the free trial early is the best way to secure your spot for the next intake.
