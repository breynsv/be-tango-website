Why You Should Learn Argentine Tango

Sonja & Sven April 1, 2020 5 min read

Argentine tango represents one of the most sophisticated couple dances you can learn. It welcomes participants of all ages and backgrounds, regardless of prior dance experience with salsa, ballroom, or swing styles.

What Makes Tango Special?

Argentine tango is more than just a dance—it's a journey of connection, creativity, and cultural discovery. Whether you're looking to improve your coordination, meet new people, or discover a new passion, tango offers something unique for everyone.

A Social Dance for Everyone

Tango functions as a social dance with regular gatherings called milongas where practitioners "practice dancing, talk, share a drink" in welcoming environments. Brussels offers nightly opportunities for social tango dancing, meaning you can go out dancing every night of the week if you wish.

These milongas create a warm community where dancers of all levels come together to share their passion for tango. It's not about perfect technique—it's about enjoying the music, the connection, and the moment.

Improvisation: The Heart of Tango

As a folkloric dance form, Argentine tango allows leaders to interpret the music and improvise the dance steps. Unlike choreographed dances where you follow set patterns, tango is an improvised conversation between two dancers and the music.

Followers then embellish movements with techniques like boleo's and gancho's, enabling dancers to "be creative and invent your own steps." This freedom of expression is what makes each dance unique and exciting.

Do I Need a Partner?

The school addresses this common concern directly: "Yes! It takes two to tango!" However, they note that "we will help you find someone to join our dance classes" and work to balance leaders and followers so "everyone gets the chance to dance with a partner."

Many dancers start without a partner and find dance partners through the classes and milongas. The tango community is welcoming and supportive, making it easy to integrate and practice with different partners.

Beyond Tango: Milonga & Vals

When you learn Argentine tango, you also discover two related dances that are played at every milonga:

  • Milonga: A "fast and rhythmical dance" with simple steps. It's playful, energetic, and fun—perfect for when you want to dance with joy and spontaneity.
  • Vals (Waltz): A "circular dance" performed continuously to "1-2-3 rhythm." It's smooth, flowing, and romantic—like floating across the dance floor.

An International Dance Community

Tango functions as an "international dance" enabling connections with "people from everywhere and of all ages." Major cities worldwide host tango parties and festivals, meaning you can dance tango anywhere you travel.

From Brussels to Buenos Aires, from Prague to Paris, you'll find a welcoming tango community ready to embrace you. The dance transcends language barriers—it's a universal form of expression that brings people together through music and movement.
